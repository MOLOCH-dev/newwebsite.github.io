Anushree Sabnis

I am a received my Bachelor's in Mechanical Engineering (2019-2023) from VJTI Mumbai .

I am a research intern working with Prof. Auke J. Ijspeert at the Biorobotics Lab, EPFL, where I was funded by the EPFL SRP. I worked on the development of a model-based optimisation framework leveraging aeroelasticity in Aerial-Aquatic Ornithopters. I am also investigating the impact of Afferent Feedback on Energy Expenditure during Human Locomotion. Prior to that, I have worked as a research intern at at the Stochastic Robotics Lab, Indian Institute of Science, advised by Prof. Shishir Kolathaya, where I worked towards the development of a virtual sensor for foot-contact detection and enhancing RL-based controllers for robust quadrupedal walking. I have also worked as a student researcher at the Multi-Robot Autonomy Laboratory @ IISER Bhopal on the development of co-operative lifting algorithms for drones and motion-planning for multi-robot drone-manipulator systems. Apart from my research activities, I also led the Society of Robotics and Automation, at VJTI. As the lead of this group, I have mentored several student-driven projects and teams in events/competitions of national and international significance.

Research Interests

I'm interested in field and assistive robotics, robot control, machine learning, optimization, and motion-planning. Much of my research is about developing mathematical models of biological systems to gain insights for optimising robot locomotion.
